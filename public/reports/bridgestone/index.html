Executive Summary

Computed from 594 real imported visits, Feb–June 2026
The clearest and most consistent trend across five months of real Mystery Shopper visits is a steady decline in Bridgestone's recommendation share: 39% of February visits, 32% in March, 17% in April, 12% in May, and 11% in June. Continental briefly overtook Bridgestone in April (21%) and May (18%) but fell sharply to just 4% in June — the real story in June is Dunlop surging to 39% recommendation share, the new brand to watch.

Unlike the deck's May-only figures suggested, the fuller real dataset shows promotion mentions and warranty offers have stayed fairly stable across all five months (promotions 40–45%, warranty offered 58–75%, actually improving to 75% in June) — the concern is specifically recommendation share, not a broader collapse in staff behaviour. Recommendation basis has held steady around 60–64% Price over Quality throughout.

Bridgestone stock availability sits at 69–80% across the period, dipping to its lowest (69%) in June — worth watching alongside the recommendation share drop. Real Competition Execution tracking (126 visits, Supa Quick channel) shows Bridgestone POSM cube and counter-flyer visibility both at 48%.

Priority action: Bridgestone recommendation training and incentives remain the top lever, with June's Dunlop surge now the most urgent competitive signal to respond to.
