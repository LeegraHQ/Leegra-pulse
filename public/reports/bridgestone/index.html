Executive Summary

From Bridgestone's May 2026 deck
The clearest and most consistent trend across four months of Mystery Shopper assessments is a steady decline in Bridgestone's recommendation share: staff recommended Bridgestone on 39% of February visits, 32% in March, 17% in April, and just 12% in May — while Continental has risen over the same period to become the most recommended brand in both April (21%) and May (18%). Bridgestone remains the focus brand of the programme, but is now being consistently out-recommended at the point of sale.

May also shows a sharp collapse in two supporting behaviours: only 1% of visits had staff mention any promotion (down from 79% in April), and no warranty was mentioned at all during May's assessments. Recommendation basis swung back toward Price (75%) over Quality (25%), consistent with staff not building enough value in the conversation before quoting.

On execution: POSM counter flyers are working well (97–100% visibility at most groups), but the Bridgestone POSM floor cube is only visible in 31% of sampled stores — Supa Quick is the standout at 100%, TWT trails at 41%, Tires & More lowest at 20%. Bridgestone stock availability overall sits at a healthy 76%, so the gap is recommendation behaviour and in-store activation, not supply.

Immediate actions Bridgestone flagged: mandatory Bridgestone recommendation training, a recommendation incentive programme, channel manager workshops, and mandatory discovery questions before any tyre recommendation.
